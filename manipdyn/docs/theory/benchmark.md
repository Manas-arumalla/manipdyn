# The benchmark suite

The centerpiece: run every controller and planner on common, reproducible
scenarios and report **measured** metrics — so the project answers *which
method wins, and when*, with data instead of adjectives.

```bash
manipdyn bench --which all          # controllers + planners -> benchmarks/results/
manipdyn bench --which controllers  # just the control zoo
```

Outputs land in `benchmarks/results/`: `BENCHMARK.md` (tables), `controllers.png`
/ `planners.png` (comparison plots), and `results.json` (raw numbers).

## Fairness

Every controller is instantiated with its **tuned gains**
([`tuned_controller`](optimization.md#tuned-presets)) and asked to reach the
**same** set of goals; all are scored in a common space — end-effector position
error toward the goal — regardless of whether they internally track a joint or
Cartesian set-point.

## Controller results (reach scenarios, `scene_base`)

| controller | final err (mm) | settle (s) | effort ‖τ‖² | peak τ (Nm) | compute (ms) |
|------------|---------------:|-----------:|------------:|------------:|-------------:|
| pid        | 0.23   | 0.26 | 6.9e3 | 875  | 0.008 |
| ctc        | 8e-10  | 0.23 | 6.0e3 | 523  | 0.013 |
| lqr        | 2e-5   | 0.34 | 2.2e3 | 352  | 0.010 |
| ilqr       | 0.01   | 0.29 | 2.2e3 | 220  | 0.12  |
| impedance  | 2.93   | 0.55 | 4.2e3 | 486  | 0.010 |
| osc        | 0.008  | 0.18 | 6.9e3 | 797  | 0.051 |
| tsid       | 0.025  | 0.20 | 2.3e3 | 233  | 1.57  |
| mppi       | 8.95   | 1.21 | 1.8e3 | 76   | 22.5  |

What the numbers say:

* **Computed-torque and LQR** are the accuracy champions (µm-scale residual) —
  exact feedback linearization / optimal linear feedback on a well-modeled arm.
* **OSC and TSID** settle fastest in task space; **TSID** does so with low
  torque and *guaranteed* limit satisfaction, but pays ~1.6 ms/step for the QP.
* **PID** is the cheapest to compute (~8 µs) and perfectly serviceable.
* **Impedance** leaves a few-mm steady-state error — expected for a
  Jacobian-transpose spring without inertia shaping.
* **MPPI** is the most expensive (~22 ms/step) and least precise here, but uses
  the *least peak torque* — the gradient-free sampler trades accuracy and
  compute for generality (and is the prime target for GPU/MJX acceleration).

## Planner results (obstacle scene)

| planner | success | plan time (s) | path len (rad) | nodes |
|---------|--------:|--------------:|---------------:|------:|
| rrt | 1.0 | 0.010 | 1.6 | 13 |
| rrt_connect | 1.0 | **0.0009** | 1.6 | 10 |
| rrt_star | 1.0 | 2.54 | 1.6 | 6 |
| informed_rrt_star | 1.0 | 13.2 | 1.6 | 6 |
| prm | 1.0 | 4.54 | 1.6 | 3 |

`rrt_connect` finds a solution ~1000× faster than the optimal planners; the
optimal variants spend their budget shrinking cost. (In this query the
straight-line path is feasible, so post-shortcut lengths converge — a more
cluttered scenario would separate them further; see future work.)

> All numbers are regenerated by `manipdyn bench` and are seed-controlled for
> reproducibility. They will be refreshed as scenarios are added.
